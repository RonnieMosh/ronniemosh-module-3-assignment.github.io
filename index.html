Chicken

Once upon a time, in a small farm nestled in the rolling hills of the countryside, there lived a testy chicken named Chuck. Chuck was a proud and cantankerous bird, and he had a reputation among the other animals on the farm as being quick to anger and slow to forgive. One day, Chuck was strutting around the chicken coop, pecking at grains of feed and clucking to himself, when he suddenly became annoyed by the noise that the other chickens were making. Chuck was a solitary bird who preferred to spend his days in peace and quiet, and the constant chatter and squawking of his feathered companions was more than he could bear. With an irritated squawk, Chuck flapped his wings and chased the other chickens out of the coop, barking orders at them as they fled in all directions. When the last of the birds had escaped, Chuck settled back down on his perch with a satisfied cluck, relishing the silence that had fallen over the coop. But Chuck's peace was short-lived, as the farmer's wife soon arrived to collect the eggs. Chuck had always resented the intrusion of the humans into his domain, and he saw the arrival of the farmer's wife as a direct threat to his authority. With a fierce squawk, Chuck charged at the woman, flapping his wings and pecking at her with his sharp beak. The farmer's wife shrieked in surprise and fled the coop, leaving Chuck to crow triumphantly from his perch. From that day on, the other animals on the farm avoided Chuck whenever possible, knowing that it was best not to cross the testy chicken. Chuck, for his part, remained aloof and solitary, content to spend his days in the peace and quiet of the chicken coop. The end.
